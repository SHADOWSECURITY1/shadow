Founder & CEO

Michael de Geus

Twelve years U.S. Secret Service, Presidential Protective Division. Six years private sector. Five hundred-plus events secured. Zero-incident record. Operations across four continents.

Michael de Geus spent twelve years as a U.S. Secret Service Special Agent on the Presidential Protective Division, the unit charged with protecting the sitting President of the United States. The work was proactive by training and doctrine: see the threat before it forms, build the plan that prevents it, brief the team that executes it.

After his Secret Service career, de Geus spent six years in the private sector running global security for an entertainment company, leading protective programs across four continents and securing more than five hundred events without a single incident.

He founded Shadow to bring that same standard to a private market that had never before had access to it. The Shadow Doctrine is the codification of how he was trained and how he has worked across nearly two decades.

Chief of Staff

He serves as Chief of Staff, owning Shadow’s operational architecture, partner standards, and senior point of escalation across international deployments.

Chief Marketing Officer

Terrell Brown

Terrell leads Shadow’s marketing strategy and brand positioning, overseeing communications, digital presence, and client engagement across all Shadow verticals. He translates the discipline and operational rigor behind Shadow’s work into language that resonates with the executives and organizations that trust us with their most critical exposure.

Chief Financial Officer

Joseph Haslam

Joseph oversees Shadow’s financial operations, contract structure, and engagement economics, ensuring every program is as sound fiscally as it is operationally. Chief Operations Officer

John Cramer

John Cramer leads Shadow’s operational infrastructure across two critical domains: product development and logistics. On the product side, he oversees the design, prototyping, and production of custom protective equipment, field gear, and specialized tactical tools, drawing on deep manufacturing relationships built through work with brands including Oakley, Leatherback Gear, and Force Multiplier. On the logistics side, he manages the procurement, inventory, and asset coordination that ensures every Shadow deployment is fully equipped and operationally ready before the first team hits the ground.

Head of App & Business Development

JP Alfred

Jean-Pierre leads Shadow’s app and business development, overseeing platform growth, operational expansion, and the technology connecting licensed professionals, agencies, and clients at scale. Director of International Business Development

Bjorn Jonsson

Bjorn Jonsson is a business executive and international growth strategist with senior leadership experience across action sports, telecommunications, optical, and consumer products. As Founder and Principal of Smoothstone Advisory, he advises public and privately held companies on growth strategy, global expansion, and go-to-market execution. Director of Command Operations

Travis Walker

Travis Walker is a retired police chief and corporate security executive with more than 30 years of experience spanning public safety, counterterrorism, crisis response, and large-scale event operations. He served as initial Incident Commander during the 2015 terrorist attack in Southern California and brings command center discipline to Shadow, providing strategic leadership across incident management, global threat assessment, and multi-agency crisis response.

Director of Artist Relations’ Security

Shaun Myers
