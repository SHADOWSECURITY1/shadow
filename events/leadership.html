Founder & CEO

Michael de Geus

Twelve years U.S. Secret Service, Presidential Protective Division. Six years private sector. Five hundred-plus events secured. Zero-incident record. Operations across four continents.

Michael de Geus spent twelve years as a U.S. Secret Service Special Agent on the Presidential Protective Division, the unit charged with protecting the sitting President of the United States. The work was anticipatory by training and doctrine: see the threat before it forms, build the plan that prevents it, brief the team that executes it.

After his Secret Service career, de Geus spent six years in the private sector running global security for an entertainment company, leading protective programs across four continents and securing more than five hundred events without a single incident.

He founded Shadow to bring that same standard to a private market that had never before had access to it. The Shadow Doctrine is the codification of how he was trained and how he has worked across nearly two decades.

Engagement Architecture

Named senior owner on every engagement.

Every Shadow engagement is led by a named member of the directorate, supported by senior agents, advance leads, and protective drivers from a vetted network. Hosts know who is accountable for the engagement before the first advance team deploys.

Standard

Trained at the highest standard.

Shadow's leadership and senior team carry training and operating experience drawn from the U.S. Secret Service, federal protective service, intelligence community, special operations, emergency medicine, and high-end private-sector security. The bar for joining the Shadow team is set by the founder's own standard, which is the standard of the unit that trained him.

The team that arrives at your event is not staffed from a roster. It is selected, briefed, and led by a member of the directorate. The advance team that walks the venue ahead of your principal is the same team that will be there on the day, applying the same plan they wrote.

That continuity is part of why the Shadow Doctrine produces the outcomes it does.
